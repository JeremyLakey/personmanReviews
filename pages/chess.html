A Deeper Look at Chess


The Game of Prestige

Chess is in a interesting place in society. On one hand, it is viewed as the ultimate game of wits. On the other, it is viewed as monotous and borning. How good of a board game is chess? Is it worth playing today with all these other boards games coming out? Why do people enjoy the game so much but others find it bland?

Skill Curves

The most postitive attribute of chess is complexity. Even computers lack a full understanding of the game. Grandmasters continue to learn, and enjoy to learn more about the game they have already mastered. Complexity is not always bad in a game. Especially the sublime complexity that comes from a handful of different pieces. When complexity is managable, it allows players to make interesting decisions. Yet there are always new things to try.

Decision Paralysis

Chess's complexity is not for everyone. Many players get stuck playing chess. Thier own minds unable to make a decision out of confusion and fraustration. This is commonly known as decision paralysis. Overcoming decision paralysis is hard because a player needs to learn. Learn takes time and is terrible at first.

The Joy of learning

When a player starts to like learning, chess is an all consuming desire. It drives people to discussion, which inspires more learning. Which inspires more playing. Sparking a cycle of learning. The entire chess community is built on getting better. Chess is simply chess. It will always be a game with a global community of learning. Thou hard to get into, it is often a well loved journey by the honest player
